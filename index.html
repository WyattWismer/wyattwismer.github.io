Experience

During the past semester of class I worked with OpenCV to develop the Whitefish Measuring Tool (WFMT), an application to assist researchers with the measurement of Whitefish. WFMT applies thresholding with minimum fitting polygons to automate standard measurements. With built in data entry and feature measurement WFMT is able to dramatically reduce the workload on researchers, increasing efficiency by 280%. This summer I am completing a CO-OP term with Hatch, developing Python based software for the Industrial Internet of Things. At Hatch there is an emphasis placed on writing robust code to handle all eventualities; in meeting this goal I have gained experience with multithreading, Linux daemons, and sockets.
